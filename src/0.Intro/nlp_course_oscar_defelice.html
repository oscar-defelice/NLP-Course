05 / My role at WhiteLab Genomics

Head of AI

Leading the design and development of AI models that power the WhiteLab platform — from sequence-level predictions to generative molecular design.

NLP / Sequence Modelling

Biological sequences are treated as languages. Transformer-based models learn the grammar of genomic sequences, enabling prediction of therapeutic properties.

ML pipeline

Active learning, graph neural networks, in-silico simulation, multi-omics integration — all feeding rational molecular design.

Why NLP & Genomics intersect

  • DNA/RNA sequences share structure with natural language — tokens, grammar, context
  • Attention mechanisms capture long-range dependencies in sequences, just as in text
  • Pre-trained genomic LLMs (Nucleotide Transformer, ESM, DNABERT) mirror GPT architectures
  • Generative models can design novel therapeutic constructs from learned distributions

07 / Course Overview — Key Concepts

What we'll cover

Tokenization

Splitting text into tokens — regex-based and byte-pair encoding (BPE).

N-gram LMs

Probabilistic models based on the Markov assumption.

Naive Bayes

Generative classifiers, class-specific unigrams, F1-score evaluation.

Logistic Regression

Discriminative models, sigmoid, gradient descent optimisation.

Embeddings

word2vec, TF-IDF — representing word meaning as dense vectors.

Feedforward NNs

Fixed context windows, generalisation via embeddings.

RNNs & GRUs

Temporal architectures, hidden states, gated context management.

Transformers

Self-attention, parallelisation, the backbone of modern LLMs.
